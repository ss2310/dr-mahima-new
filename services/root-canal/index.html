What Happens During a Root Canal?

Visit 1

The Relief (45–60 Minutes)

  1. Diagnosis: We take a digital X-ray to see the shape of the roots.
  2. Numbing: We anesthetize the tooth.
  3. Access: A tiny opening is made in the top of the tooth.
  4. Cleaning: Using fine files, we remove the infected nerve (pulp) and bacteria from the canals.
  5. Sealing: The empty canals are filled with Gutta Percha to seal them perfectly.
  6. Filling: A temporary filling closes the opening. (Pain is gone by this point!)

Visit 2

The Protection (3–5 Days Later)

  1. Checkup: We ensure there is no sensitivity.
  2. Measurement: We take an impression for your Crown.
  3. Fixing: The permanent Crown is cemented onto the tooth. You are ready to eat normally!
