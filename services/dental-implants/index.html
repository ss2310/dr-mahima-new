Why Does the Price Vary? (₹15k vs ₹50k)

Not all dental implants are the same. The price variation depends on three main choices you make with your dentist:

1. The Implant Brand

Budget (₹15,000 – ₹22,000): FDA-approved Korean or Israeli brands (e.g., Osstem, Adin, MIS). Excellent track record with 93–95% success rates. Ideal for single back teeth where aesthetics are less critical.

Mid-Range (₹25,000 – ₹35,000): Established international brands offering faster healing and better surface technology. Good for most cases.

Premium (₹40,000 – ₹50,000): Straumann (Switzerland) or Nobel Biocare (Sweden) — the global gold standard. 97%+ success rate and faster osseointegration. Recommended for front teeth and complex cases.

2. The Crown Material

PFM — Porcelain Fused to Metal (₹3,000 – ₹5,000): Strong and affordable. Has a metal substructure that can show a thin grey line at the gumline over time. Best for back teeth.

Zirconia — Metal-Free (₹5,000 – ₹12,000): The gold standard in 2026. 100% natural appearance, extremely durable (1,200 MPa flexural strength), biocompatible, and stain-resistant. Recommended for front teeth.

3. Case Complexity

Simple case (single tooth, good bone): Straightforward placement, lower cost, faster healing.

Complex case (multiple teeth, bone loss, sinus proximity): May require bone grafting, sinus lift, or guided surgery — adding ₹5,000–₹25,000 to the total.

Who Should (and Shouldn't) Get Dental Implants?

✓ Good Candidates

Adults 18+ with one or more missing teeth. Patients with healthy gums (or treated gum disease). People who want a permanent, maintenance-free solution. Patients with adequate bone density (or willing to undergo bone grafting). Denture wearers tired of removable teeth. Patients with controlled diabetes, hypertension, or other managed chronic conditions.

✗ Not Recommended For

Heavy smokers unwilling to quit (reduces success rate to ~85%). Patients with uncontrolled diabetes (HbA1c above 8%). Patients currently undergoing radiation therapy to the jaw area. Children and teenagers whose jawbones are still developing. Patients with severe untreated gum disease (must be treated first).

Your Complete Implant Journey (3–6 Months)

Here is exactly what happens at each stage so you know what to expect:

Step 1

Free Consultation & 3D Scan (Day 1)

We perform a CBCT (3D X-ray) to assess bone density, nerve positions, and sinus proximity. Dr. Mahima creates a digital treatment plan and gives you an exact cost estimate. This visit is free with no obligation.

Step 2

Implant Placement Surgery (Day 7–14)

The titanium implant is placed into the jawbone under local anesthesia. It takes 45–60 minutes per implant. Most patients report less discomfort than a tooth extraction. A temporary tooth is placed if needed — you never leave our clinic without a tooth.

Step 3

Healing & Osseointegration (3 Months)

The titanium implant fuses with your natural jawbone. During this period you eat normally with your temporary tooth. We schedule checkups at 1 week, 1 month, and 3 months to monitor healing.

Step 4

Abutment Placement (Month 3)

Once the implant is fully bonded, we attach the abutment (connector) and take impressions for your permanent crown. This is a painless 20-minute procedure.

Step 5

Final Crown Fitting (Month 3–4)

Your custom Zirconia or PFM crown is cemented onto the abutment. We check your bite, adjust if needed, and you walk out with a permanent tooth that looks, feels, and functions exactly like a natural tooth.

Dental Implant Aftercare Guide

Proper care ensures your implant lasts a lifetime. Here's what to do:

First 7 Days After Surgery

Eat soft foods only (dal, khichdi, curd, soup, mashed vegetables). Apply ice pack externally — 15 minutes on, 15 minutes off — to reduce swelling. Rinse gently with warm salt water 3–4 times daily. Take all prescribed antibiotics and painkillers on time. Avoid spitting forcefully, using straws, or smoking. Sleep with your head slightly elevated on two pillows.

Long-Term Care (Lifetime)

Brush twice daily with a soft-bristled toothbrush. Floss daily — a water flosser (Waterpik) works excellently around implants. Visit Dr. Mahima's every 6 months for professional cleaning and implant checkup. Avoid chewing ice, hard candy, sugarcane, or using teeth to open packages. If you grind your teeth at night, ask about a protective night guard.
